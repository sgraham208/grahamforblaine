Canvass Script

Start
Before You Knock

Pre-Door Checklist

  • Palm card in hand
  • Check voter name on app
  • Phone charged, $1 QR ready
  • grahamforblaine.com/sign bookmarked

Approach from the street side. Smile. Relaxed posture. You're a neighbor, not a salesman.

You are the sitting Clerk showing respect by showing up. The ask is simple: keep doing the job.

The unaffiliated voter is your margin. The open primary is the mechanism. The learning curve argument is the closer.

"Confidence without theater."
The Knock

Opening

Knock firmly, three times. Step back. If there's a Ring doorbell, look at it and smile.

"Hi — I'm Stephen Graham. I'm your Blaine County Clerk, and I'm running for re-election in the May primary. I'm out meeting voters today. Do you have a quick minute?"
Conversation — 60-90 sec max

The Bridge Question

"Is there anything about the Clerk's office — elections, courts, public records, the county budget — that matters to you or that you'd like to know more about?"

Then shut up and listen. If they already know something, acknowledge it — don't recite the talking point.

What did they raise?

Or jump to a situation:

Topic Response

Elections / Voting

"Good — that's the one that keeps me up at night. We've run the highest voter turnout in the state. Every ballot counted, every deadline met, every result certified on time. I take that personally."
If they push toward election-denier territory
"Blaine County's elections are transparent, auditable, and accurate. I'm proud of that and I'll defend it. But I try to stay focused on the work, not the noise."
Topic Response

Budget / Taxes

"I'm the county's budget officer — that's a statutory role most people don't know the Clerk has. We're replacing a twenty-year-old system with modern financial software — the biggest upgrade in county history. It goes live this summer. That's not the kind of project you hand off midstream."
If they ask about the system
"Tyler Technologies — it's the industry standard for municipal ERP. We're migrating everything: general ledger, accounts payable, payroll, budgeting. On schedule for summer go-live."
If they ask about transition risk
"I'll be transparent — there will be transition bumps. There always are with a migration this size. What I can tell you is I'm managing it daily and we've stayed within budget."
If they press on property taxes
"I hear you. The Board sets the budget, the Assessor determines values, the state provides the formula, and I'm the one who runs the calculation and certifies the levy. I don't set the budget and I don't set the values — but I make sure the math is right. We've held our own office costs flat while modernizing systems that were two decades out of date."
If they push further — "what about the rest of the county?"
"The Clerk puts the recommended budget together for every county department — not just my own. I've sent department budgets back for revision before forwarding them to the Commissioners. The budget officer role exists to be an independent check, and I take that seriously."
Topic Response

Courts / Legal

"We run court operations for the Fifth Judicial District here in Blaine County. My team handles caseloads that peer counties staff at significantly higher levels — and they do it well. That's something I'm proud of."
Topic Response

Recording / Property

"Every deed, every lien, every mortgage recorded in Blaine County comes through our office. Title companies depend on us. We've modernized those systems and the feedback from the industry has been strong."
If they work in title/real estate and already noticed
"That's great to hear — we've worked hard on that. The recording side doesn't get a lot of attention, but it matters to the people who use it every day."
Topic Response

Nothing Specific

Read the room — homeowner vs. younger renter.

"That's fair — most people don't think about the Clerk's office until they need it. If you've ever voted, served on a jury, bought a house, or paid property taxes here, my office was part of that. I've spent four years making sure those systems work well. I'd love your support in the May 19 primary."
Young / renter variant
"That's fair — most people don't think about the Clerk's office until they need it. If you've ever voted here, my office ran that election. And if you care about whether this valley stays affordable, the county budget is where that starts — I'm the one who builds it. I'd love your support in the May 19 primary."
Topic Response

Not Clerk's Office

Roads, water, schools, housing — listen, connect if you can, don't pretend you can fix it.

"That's not something the Clerk's office handles directly — but as budget officer, the funding for that flows through the budget I put together. I hear you that it matters."
Situation

They Mention the Opponent

Do not name Cieslik. Do not attack. Pivot to record. If pressed twice, hold the line.
"I don't think this job benefits from a learning curve right now. We're mid-implementation on a major project. I want to land it."
Lighter version (casual, barely-curious mention)
"Yeah, there's a primary — that's how it should work. I'm focused on finishing what I started."
"Pick one thing" question
"Modernizing the county's financial systems. We were running on a platform from the early 2000s. The replacement goes live this summer. And the most important thing ahead is landing that implementation — new system, trained staff, clean data — and then using it to give the public real visibility into county finances."
Situation

Party ID Came Up

Party ID

Unaffiliated / Independent

This voter can vote in the Democratic primary with zero friction. The Democratic ballot is the ONLY partisan primary they can participate in without affiliating. Frame it as a gift, not a request.
"Actually, that works in your favor here. The Republican primary is closed — you'd have to register Republican to vote in it. But the Democratic primary is open to everyone. You just ask for the ballot. The County Clerk is a nonpartisan job in practice — courts, elections, budgets, public records. I'd be honored to have your support."
Party ID

Registered Republican

You're asking them to choose your ballot over their party's. Be lighter. Plant the seed. Don't push.
"I appreciate that. Idaho's Democratic primary is open — any registered voter can request a Democratic ballot without changing their registration. The County Clerk is a nonpartisan job in practice: courts, elections, budgets, public records. I'd be honored to have your support regardless of party. But either way, thanks for your time."
Situation

County Employee

Name the dynamic. Don't pretend the power relationship isn't there.

"I appreciate that — and I know this is a little different when you work for the county. I'm not here as your boss's boss. I'm here as a candidate asking for your vote. If that's uncomfortable, I'll just leave a card and move on."
Don't ask county employees for yard signs, volunteer commitments, or public endorsements. A card and a thank-you is enough.
Strong Supporter

The Ask Sequence

Work through these in order. Read the room — skip what doesn't fit.

"That means a lot — thank you."
1. Yard Sign
"Would you be willing to put a yard sign in your yard? It makes a real difference — people see a name in their neighbor's yard, it sticks."

If yes → "I'll get one to you as soon as they're ready." Log name + address.

2. Endorsement

Best for longtime residents, community leaders, business owners. Read the room.

"One more thing — would you be comfortable lending your name as an endorser? I'm putting together a list of community members who support my re-election. It could be as simple as your name on a list, or if you're up for it, a short letter to the Mountain Express."

If yes → note name, phone/email, name-only or letter. Follow up within a week.
If hesitant → "No pressure at all. Think about it — my email's on the card."

3. $1 Grassroots Ask
"And if you want to chip in, even a dollar keeps this campaign going."

Show QR code on phone. "Scan that and you're done."

4. Close
"Mention my name to a neighbor or two — word of mouth is everything in a race like this. Early voting starts April 27. If you won't be around, you can request an absentee ballot through May 8 at VoteIdaho.gov."

Hand palm card. Log as strong supporter.

If they offer to donate: "Thank you — the website has everything: grahamforblaine.com."
Checks: Graham for Blaine, PO Box 395, Hailey, ID 83333

Wrap Up

Graceful Exit

"I appreciate your time. Here's a card with my info — the QR code goes right to my website. Early voting starts April 27, and if you won't be around, you can request an absentee ballot through May 8 at VoteIdaho.gov. Primary is May 19. Thanks for talking with me."

Hand the palm card. Smile. Walk away clean.

No Answer

Leave a Door Hanger

Write in the writable strip. Keep it short and personal. Use their name if it's on your list.

"Sorry I missed you — Stephen Graham, your County Clerk. Hope to earn your vote May 19!"
"Stopped by to say hello. I'd love to hear what matters to you — info@grahamforblaine.com"
"[Name] — thanks for being a voter. Hope to connect soon. — Stephen"
Situation

Hostile / Not Interested

"I understand. Thanks for your time — have a good day."

Walk away. Don't engage. Don't argue. Log in MiniVAN and move on.

Never argue at a door. Never stay longer than 2 minutes. Never bad-mouth anyone. Never talk national politics. Never promise anything outside the Clerk's office.
